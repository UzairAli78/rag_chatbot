SECTION 1: COMPANY OVERVIEW & VALUES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 About Apex Solutions
Apex Solutions Pvt. Ltd. is a technology and consulting firm founded in 2010,
headquartered in Karachi, Pakistan, with branch offices in Lahore and Islamabad.
The company specializes in software development, IT consulting, and digital
transformation services.

1.2 Mission Statement
To deliver innovative, high-quality technology solutions that empower businesses
to grow and succeed in the digital era.

1.3 Core Values
- Integrity: We act with honesty and transparency in everything we do.
- Excellence: We strive for the highest standards in our work and service.
- Collaboration: We believe the best outcomes emerge from teamwork.
- Innovation: We encourage creative thinking and continuous improvement.
- Respect: We treat every individual with dignity and fairness.

1.4 Policy Applicability
This handbook applies to all full-time employees, part-time employees, contract
staff, and interns at Apex Solutions Pvt. Ltd. unless otherwise specified.

2.4 Reporting Structure
Every employee has a direct Line Manager (LM) and a dotted-line relationship
with the HR Business Partner assigned to their department. Employees should
direct day-to-day work queries to their LM and HR-related queries to their
assigned HRBP.

2.5 Promotions
Promotions are based on performance ratings, tenure, skill assessments, and
business need. Employees are eligible for promotion review after a minimum of
12 months in their current role. Promotions are not automatic and must be
approved by the department head and HR.

SECTION 3: RECRUITMENT & ONBOARDING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Recruitment Process
All hiring is managed by the HR department in collaboration with the hiring
manager. The standard recruitment process is:

  Step 1: Job requisition submitted by department head.
  Step 2: HR posts the vacancy on approved job boards and internal portals.
  Step 3: CV screening by HR Recruitment Specialist.
  Step 4: Technical/aptitude assessment (if applicable).
  Step 5: First interview with HR (behavioral/cultural fit).
  Step 6: Second interview with department head or panel.
  Step 7: Reference checks conducted by HR.
  Step 8: Offer letter issued by HR upon approval.
  Step 9: Offer acceptance and documentation.

3.2 Internal Job Postings
All open positions are first advertised internally for a minimum of 5 business
days before external sourcing begins. Employees with at least 12 months of
service are eligible to apply for internal positions.

3.3 Employee Referrals
Employees may refer candidates for open positions. A referral bonus of PKR 10,000
is payable to the referring employee if the referred candidate is hired and
completes 6 months of continuous employment.

3.4 Onboarding
New employees undergo a structured onboarding program:

  Day 1:
    - Welcome meeting with HR
    - Office tour and workstation setup
    - IT equipment issuance and system access
    - Employee ID card and access badge issuance

  Week 1:
    - HR orientation: policies, benefits, and code of conduct
    - Department introduction and team meetings
    - IT security and data privacy training

  Month 1:
    - Buddy system: each new hire is assigned an onboarding buddy
    - Role-specific training plan initiated by Line Manager
    - 30-day check-in with HR

  Month 3:
    - Probation mid-point review with Line Manager
    - 90-day check-in with HR

3.5 Documents Required at Joining
  - Original CNIC (Computerized National Identity Card) and 2 photocopies
  - Educational certificates (originals for verification)
  - Previous employment experience letters
  - 2 passport-size photographs
  - Bank account details for payroll
  - Emergency contact information form

SECTION 4: WORKING HOURS & ATTENDANCE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Standard Working Hours
The official working week at Apex Solutions is Monday to Friday.
Standard office hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (local time), inclusive of a
1-hour lunch break from 1:00 PM to 2:00 PM.
Total working hours per day: 8 hours (excluding lunch break).
Total working hours per week: 40 hours.

4.2 Flexible Working Hours (Flextime)
Employees in Tier 3 and above, and those in roles approved by HR, may opt for
flexible start times between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM, provided they complete
their 8-hour workday and are available during core hours of 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM.
Flextime arrangements must be approved in writing by the Line Manager and HR.

4.3 Attendance Policy
- Employees are required to mark attendance via the biometric system upon arrival
  and departure each day.
- Failure to mark attendance without a valid reason will be recorded as
  Absent Without Leave (AWOL) for that day.
- Three consecutive AWOL days may result in termination of employment.
- Late arrival is defined as arriving more than 10 minutes after the scheduled
  start time. Three late arrivals in a calendar month equal one absence.

4.4 Overtime
Overtime is work performed beyond 8 hours per day or 40 hours per week.
Overtime must be pre-approved by the Line Manager.
- Tier 4 and Tier 5 employees: Compensated at 1.5x the hourly rate.
- Tier 3 employees: Compensatory time off (CTO) granted on a 1:1 basis.
- Tier 1 and Tier 2 employees: Overtime is considered part of their overall
  compensation package and is not separately remunerated.

4.5 Public Holidays
The company observes all public holidays declared by the Government of Pakistan.
The HR department publishes the official holiday calendar at the beginning of
each year. Typically, employees receive 14–16 paid public holidays annually.

4.6 Weekend Work
Saturday and Sunday are non-working days. In exceptional cases, employees
required to work on weekends will receive compensatory time off or overtime pay
as per Section 4.4. Weekend work requires prior approval from the department head.

SECTION 5: SALARY, COMPENSATION & PAYROLL
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Salary Structure
All salaries are denominated in Pakistani Rupees (PKR) and structured as a
monthly gross salary comprising the following components:

  Basic Salary:        50% of Gross Salary
  House Rent Allowance (HRA):  25% of Basic Salary
  Conveyance Allowance:        PKR 5,000 (fixed, all tiers)
  Medical Allowance:           10% of Basic Salary
  Special Allowance:           Remaining balance to make up gross salary

5.2 Salary Bands by Tier (Monthly Gross)

  Tier 1 – Executive Leadership:   PKR 500,000 – PKR 1,500,000+
  Tier 2 – Senior Management:      PKR 250,000 – PKR 499,999
  Tier 3 – Mid-Level Management:   PKR 120,000 – PKR 249,999
  Tier 4 – Junior/Associate Staff: PKR 50,000  – PKR 119,999
  Tier 5 – Support Staff/Interns:  PKR 25,000  – PKR 49,999

Note: Actual salary within the band is determined by experience, qualifications,
performance, and market benchmarks at the time of hiring or salary revision.

5.3 Payroll Processing
- Salaries are processed on the 28th of each month.
- If the 28th falls on a weekend or public holiday, salary is processed on
  the last working day before the 28th.
- Salaries are credited directly to the employee's designated bank account.
- Monthly payslips are generated and accessible through the HR self-service portal.

5.4 Salary Deductions
The following deductions may apply to monthly salary:
  - Income Tax (as per applicable FBR tax slabs for the current fiscal year)
  - EOBI (Employees' Old-Age Benefits Institution): 1% of minimum wage
  - SESSI/PESSI (Social Security): as per provincial requirements
  - Unpaid leave or AWOL deductions
  - Loan repayment installments (if applicable)

5.5 Annual Salary Increment
- Salary increments are reviewed annually in July.
- Increments are merit-based and linked to the annual performance review.
- Standard increment range: 10% to 25% of current gross salary.
- Employees rated "Below Expectations" in their annual review are not eligible
  for an increment in that cycle.
- Increment letters are issued by HR and require acknowledgment within 7 days.

5.6 Performance Bonus
- Annual performance bonuses are disbursed in December.
- Bonus range: 1 month to 3 months of gross salary, depending on individual
  performance rating and company profitability.
  - "Exceptional" rating: Up to 3 months gross salary
  - "Exceeds Expectations" rating: Up to 2 months gross salary
  - "Meets Expectations" rating: Up to 1 month gross salary
  - "Below Expectations" rating: No bonus
- Employees must be on active payroll as of December 31 to receive the bonus.
- Employees serving notice period are not eligible for the performance bonus.

5.7 Eid Bonus
All employees who have completed at least 6 months of service receive an Eid
bonus equivalent to 1 month's basic salary, disbursed before Eid ul-Fitr.
A second Eid bonus of 0.5 months' basic salary may be disbursed before Eid ul-Adha
at the management's discretion.

5.8 Salary Advance
Employees may request a salary advance of up to 50% of their monthly gross
salary. A maximum of 2 salary advances are permitted per calendar year.
The advance will be recovered in 2 equal installments from subsequent salaries.
Requests must be submitted to HR through the HR portal and approved by the
Line Manager and Finance department.

5.9 Expense Reimbursements
Business-related expenses are reimbursed upon submission of original receipts
and a completed expense report, approved by the Line Manager. Reimbursements
are processed within 7 working days of approval.

SECTION 6: PROBATION PERIOD POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

6.1 Duration
All new employees (excluding re-hires and transfers) serve a probation period of
3 months from their date of joining.

6.2 Probation Extension
If performance during probation is deemed unsatisfactory, the probation period
may be extended by up to 3 additional months. The employee will be notified in
writing at least 7 days before the original probation end date.

6.3 Confirmation
Upon successful completion of probation, the Line Manager submits a probation
completion form to HR, and the employee receives a confirmation letter within
7 working days.

6.4 Leave During Probation
Probationary employees are entitled to:
  - Sick Leave: up to 5 days
  - Casual Leave: up to 3 days
  - Annual Leave is not applicable during probation (begins accruing after
    confirmation).

6.5 Termination During Probation
Either party may terminate employment during probation with 2 weeks written
notice or payment in lieu of notice.

SECTION 7: LEAVE POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

7.1 Annual Leave (Earned Leave)
- Full-time confirmed employees are entitled to 18 days of paid annual leave
  per calendar year.
- Annual leave accrues at 1.5 days per month starting from the confirmation date.
- A maximum of 9 days may be carried forward to the next calendar year.
- Carried-forward leave expires on March 31 of the following year.
- Annual leave must be applied for at least 3 working days in advance through
  the HR portal and approved by the Line Manager.

7.2 Sick Leave
- All confirmed employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year.
- Sick leave does not carry forward to the next calendar year.
- For sick leave exceeding 2 consecutive days, a medical certificate from a
  registered doctor is required.
- Sick leave cannot be taken as an extension to annual leave.

7.3 Casual Leave
- All confirmed employees are entitled to 8 days of paid casual leave per year.
- Casual leave is for urgent or unforeseen personal matters.
- No more than 2 consecutive casual leave days may be taken at a time.
- Casual leave does not carry forward.

7.4 Maternity Leave
- Female employees who have completed at least 6 months of service are entitled
  to 12 weeks (84 days) of fully paid maternity leave.
- Maternity leave must commence no earlier than 4 weeks before the expected
  delivery date.
- A medical certificate specifying the expected delivery date must be submitted
  to HR at least 4 weeks before commencement of leave.
- Maternity leave may be extended by up to 4 weeks as unpaid leave upon request.
- Employees are guaranteed the same or equivalent position upon return.

7.5 Paternity Leave
- Male employees who have completed at least 6 months of service are entitled
  to 10 days of paid paternity leave, to be taken within 30 days of the child's
  birth.
- A birth registration document must be submitted to HR upon return.

7.6 Bereavement Leave
In the event of the death of an immediate family member, employees are entitled
to paid bereavement leave as follows:
  - Spouse, child, or parent: 5 days
  - Sibling, grandparent, or in-law: 3 days
  - Other relatives: 1 day
Supporting documentation may be requested by HR.

7.7 Marriage Leave
Employees are entitled to 5 days of paid marriage leave once during their
tenure, applicable upon their own marriage. A marriage certificate must be
submitted to HR within 30 days.

7.8 Hajj Leave
Muslim employees who have completed 5 or more years of service are entitled to
up to 30 days of paid Hajj leave once in their entire tenure at the company.
Application must be submitted at least 60 days in advance.

7.9 Study/Exam Leave
Employees pursuing approved professional certifications or higher education
relevant to their role may apply for up to 5 days of study leave per year.
Approval is subject to business needs and must be endorsed by the Line Manager
and HR. Supporting enrollment documents are required.

7.10 Leave Without Pay (LWP)
Employees who have exhausted all applicable leave entitlements may apply for
Leave Without Pay. LWP requires approval from the department head and HR.
LWP periods do not count toward service tenure for increment or benefit purposes.

7.11 Leave Application Process
All leave applications must be submitted through the HR self-service portal.
Emergency leave may be communicated via phone or message to the Line Manager,
followed by a portal application on the next working day.
Absence without approved leave will be treated as AWOL and result in salary
deduction and possible disciplinary action.

SECTION 8: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

8.1 Performance Review Cycle
Apex Solutions follows a bi-annual performance review system:
  - Mid-Year Review: July (for feedback and goal adjustment)
  - Annual Review: December/January (for ratings, increments, and bonuses)

8.2 Performance Rating Scale
  5 – Exceptional:         Consistently surpasses all goals; role model.
  4 – Exceeds Expectations: Regularly goes beyond required performance.
  3 – Meets Expectations:   Consistently meets all job requirements.
  2 – Needs Improvement:    Partially meets expectations; improvement required.
  1 – Below Expectations:   Fails to meet minimum job requirements.

8.3 Goal Setting (OKRs)
At the start of each year, employees and their Line Managers collaboratively
define Objectives and Key Results (OKRs). Goals must be SMART:
Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

8.4 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
Employees rated 2 or below for two consecutive reviews are placed on a formal
Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). The PIP outlines:
  - Clear performance expectations
  - Timeline for improvement (typically 60–90 days)
  - Support and resources provided
  - Consequences of failure to improve
Failure to meet PIP targets may result in termination.

8.5 360-Degree Feedback
Senior staff (Tier 2 and above) undergo 360-degree feedback, which includes
input from peers, direct reports, and cross-functional stakeholders.

SECTION 9: TRAINING & DEVELOPMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

9.1 Learning & Development Budget
Each confirmed employee is allocated an annual Learning & Development (L&D)
budget as follows:
  - Tier 1 & 2: PKR 150,000 per year
  - Tier 3:     PKR 75,000 per year
  - Tier 4:     PKR 40,000 per year
  - Tier 5:     PKR 15,000 per year

This budget covers approved courses, certifications, workshops, conferences,

SECTION 15: GRIEVANCE & DISCIPLINARY POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

15.1 Grievance Procedure
An employee who has a work-related complaint or concern should follow these steps:

  Step 1 – Informal Resolution: Raise the issue verbally with the Line Manager.
           Timeline: Within 5 working days of the incident.
  Step 2 – Formal Grievance: If unresolved, submit a written grievance to HR.
           HR will acknowledge receipt within 2 working days.
  Step 3 – Investigation: HR investigates and may convene a Grievance Committee.
           Investigation period: Up to 15 working days.
  Step 4 – Decision: A written decision is communicated to the employee.
  Step 5 – Appeal: The employee may appeal to the Chief People Officer within
           5 working days of the decision.

15.2 Disciplinary Process
Minor Misconduct (first occurrence): Verbal warning documented by HR.
Minor Misconduct (second occurrence): Written warning issued by HR.
Minor Misconduct (third occurrence) or Major Misconduct: Show-cause notice,
  hearing, and possible suspension or termination.

Examples of Minor Misconduct:
  - Repeated tardiness
  - Dress code violations
  - Failure to follow reasonable instructions

Examples of Major Misconduct (may result in immediate termination):
  - Theft or fraud
  - Physical or verbal violence
  - Harassment or discrimination
  - Sharing confidential company information without authorization
  - Falsification of records
  - Substance abuse on premises

15.3 Right to Representation
During any formal disciplinary hearing, the employee has the right to be
accompanied by a colleague of their choosing (not an external legal representative).

  - Tier 1 & 2: 3 months

  - Probationary employees: 2 weeks

16.2 Full & Final Settlement
The full and final settlement, including all outstanding salary, leave encashment,

Unutilized annual leave balance (up to the maximum carry-forward of 9 days) is

Before final settlement, the exiting employee must:
  - Return all company property (laptop, ID card, access badge, keys)
  - Complete a knowledge transfer as directed by the Line Manager
  - Clear outstanding financial dues or advances
  - Complete the exit interview with HR

16.5 Termination by Company
The company reserves the right to terminate employment for:
  - Gross misconduct (without notice)
  - Poor performance after a failed PIP
  - Redundancy (with one month's notice or payment in lieu, plus severance as
    per applicable law)
  - Prolonged illness (beyond applicable leave entitlements)

16.6 Severance Pay
In cases of redundancy or company-initiated termination (non-disciplinary):
  - 1–3 years of service: 1 month gross salary per year of service
  - 3–5 years of service: 1.5 months gross salary per year of service
  - 5+ years of service: 2 months gross salary per year of service

16.7 Reference Letters
The company will provide a factual reference letter to departing employees who
have completed their notice period and exit clearance in good standing.
